1500 SAT, 7.5 IELTS, and A level Achieving my SAT and IELTS scores marked the first step in my application journey. Although I initially struggled, especially with language learning after years of focusing on science, I came to recognize the importance of foreign language proficiency in a globalized world. This understanding motivated me to persist and ultimately reach my goals. While the scores may not be exceptional, they reflect both my abilities and a meaningful learning journey. In early 2025, I began studying A Level Mathematics (9709) for the May–June session, completing AS papers in Pure Mathematics 1 (P1) and Statistics 1 (S1), achieving 82% (grade A). Although this result did not fully meet my expectations, the preparation process broadened my approach to mathematical problem-solving by integrating methods from both the A Level curriculum and my national education system. During the summer of 2025, I continued preparing for the A2 papers (P3 and M1) as well as Further Mathematics (9231). I am currently awaiting the results and will update this section accordingly.
Gold mendel in AI-JAM US competition with the Next-Generation Comprehensive Smart Home Security Monitoring System
I joined a team of six to participate in this competition in the summer of 2025, initially as the only member with no prior coding experience. I began learning programming independently through Codelearn.io, starting with Python and later C, before working with ESP32 microcontrollers and sensors such as DHT22 and PZEM004T.Through trial and error, and with guidance from my tutor, I learned to debug logical issues and improve my code, which I later documented on my personal GitHub. I was also introduced to mobile application development and AI training. Although I did not build an independent application, I gained a solid understanding of backend logic through MIT App Inventor, a block-based programming platform.
Personal research on on a smart electronic clock

Click here to see the report of this activity

I conducted this project after spending some time with the AI-JAM team, so I had accumulated more experience before starting this activity. However, this time I was responsible for every aspect of the project, including software, firmware, and hardware. I learned how to use Altium Designer to create schematic files and convert them into PCB designs. The project involved ESP32 and PZEM004T components, combined with Hilink modules, relays, and a TFT screen. I made several mistakes during the development process, but through those experiences, I learned how to troubleshoot and improve my design. Later, I trained an AI model using my own documents and used it as a learning companion to help explain my code and refine my understanding.

Mathematics class assistant Working as a mathematics class assistant gave me the opportunity to earn income for my personal needs. Although the earnings were modest, I used the entire amount to purchase books and pay for physics courses. This experience not only allowed me to apply my knowledge in a practical setting, but also marked my first step toward independence. Through this role, I learned to take greater responsibility for my studies and developed a deeper appreciation for the educational opportunities available to me.
Being Intern in Son Ha Xanh group joint stock company

During my internship, I had the opportunity to participate in the development of the solar-powered water heater project, Model “Sonha 68.” I learned how to manage operational efficiency by counting the number of outputs and comparing them to the predicted results based on the input amounts. Efficiency checks must be implemented at every stage to minimize waste and production errors. If any issues arise that result in substandard quality at a particular stage, I am required to report them to the manager for resolution.
